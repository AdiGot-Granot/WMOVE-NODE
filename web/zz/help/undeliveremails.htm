The information below is associated with http://help.myway.com/email/bounce.html

Each undelivered email may have a different reason and explanation. If you received an undelivered email that contains one of the following messages, find the description below for more information.

Most Common Undelivered Email Reasons:
• Message rejected: The mailserver used does not match the domain specified
• 450: Recipient address rejected: Domain not found
• 450: unknown: Client host rejected: Too many mails sent!
• 500: Recipient address rejected: Recipient mailbox is full
• 503: Improper use of SMTP command pipelining
• 540: Recipient address rejected: Your email has been returned because the intended recipient's email account has been suspended.
• 550: User unknown
• 550: name.mail.domain.net: Client host rejected: More than X concurrent connections per subnet
• 554: unknown: Client host rejected: Access denied
• 554: Client host rejected: cannot find your hostname
• 554: Sender address rejected: Access denied
• 554: Service unavailable; IP was blocked using relays.ordb.org. Reason: This mail was handled by an open relay
• 554: Relay access denied; reject: header Subject: (Email Subject Line)
• Remote host said: 554 Service unavailable; blocked using blackholes.myway.com

explanation of bounced messages

450: Recipient address rejected: Domain not found

450 unknown: Client host rejected: Too many mails sent!
The mail servers that were responsible for delivering the message have been blocked due to a high number of concurrent connections. This type of abuse is usually indicative of a "spammer" trying to send unsolicited commercial emails. The email provider in question has been added to a block list, this bounced message will continue.

500: Recipient address rejected: Recipient mailbox is full
The Email address in question is over its storage limit, emails sent to that account will be "bounced" back to the sender until the space has been cleared.

503: Improper use of SMTP command pipelining
The message was sent in a non-conventional, non-standard way.

540: Recipient address rejected: Your email has been returned because the intended recipient's email account has been suspended. The account must be re-activated to receive incoming messages.
Email accounts that have not been accessed for more than 60 days may be deactivated.
Once you reactivated your email, it may take up to two hours for you to begin receiving new email messages.
If you are not able to reactivate your account, we suggest that you find an alternate means of communicating with your email provider.

550: User unknown
It could mean that the email account does not exist, not active, or the email address was typed incorrectly.

550: Client host rejected: More than 20 concurrent connections per subnet (See 450 unknown)

554: Client host rejected: Access denied

554: Sender address rejected: Access denied
Your message is being blocked because it either had characteristics similar to "spam", or you're sending your message from an ISP or email provider who has been identified as allowing spam to be sent from their facilities.

554: Service unavailable;blocked using relays.ordb.org, reason: This mail was handled by an open relay
The message in question was blocked because it came from a domain or IP address that is currently listed with one of Spam filters your message will not be delivered.

554: Client host rejected: cannot find your hostname

554: Relay access denied;
This error means that the person sending the email wasn't authorized to use the email server (SMTP) to send email.

554: reject: header Subject: (Email Subject Line Here)
This subject line is on a "black list" of subject lines.

Remote host said: 554 Service unavailable; blocked using blackholes.myway.com
Due to a large number of abuse complaints regarding your account (and/or your ISP), your email have being bounced back to you.
